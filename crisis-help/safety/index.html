🛡️ Safety Checklist

Step-by-step emergency preparedness guide for people in or near the conflict zone.

Back to Crisis Help Hub

📄 Documents & Identification

Secure your identity documents — they are your most important asset in a crisis.

  • Keep your passport on your person at all times (not in luggage)
  • Take photos of your passport, visa, and ID — store in cloud (Google Drive, iCloud)
  • Email copies of all documents to yourself and a trusted family member
  • Carry printed copies in a waterproof bag separate from originals
  • Note down your embassy's emergency phone number on paper (phones can die)
  • Keep travel insurance documents accessible — know your policy number
  • Have your blood type written down and carry any medical records

📡 Communication Plan

Stay connected with family and authorities. Have backup communication methods.

  • Designate one family member as your emergency contact point
  • Set up a check-in schedule (e.g., message every 6 hours)
  • Download offline messaging apps (Briar, Bridgefy) that work without internet
  • Get a local SIM card or eSIM for the country you're in
  • Install a VPN — internet may be censored or monitored in some areas
  • Save important contacts offline — don't rely solely on cloud contacts
  • Know how to use WhatsApp, Signal, and Telegram — they work in most regions
  • Mark yourself "Safe" on Facebook Safety Check if activated for the event

🎒 Emergency Kit

Pack a go-bag that you can grab in under 2 minutes.

  • Water bottles (at least 2 liters per person)
  • Non-perishable food (energy bars, nuts, dried fruit) for 48 hours
  • Portable power bank (fully charged, 20,000mAh+ recommended)
  • Phone charging cables (USB-C and Lightning)
  • Flashlight or headlamp with extra batteries
  • First aid kit (bandages, antiseptic, pain relievers, any prescription meds)
  • Cash in multiple currencies (USD, EUR, local currency)
  • Dust mask or N95 respirator
  • Whistle (for signaling if trapped)
  • Change of clothes and sturdy shoes

🏠 Shelter-in-Place Procedures

If you cannot evacuate, follow these steps to stay safe where you are.

  • Move to an interior room away from windows and exterior walls
  • If available, go to a designated safe room or bomb shelter
  • Stay below window level — sit or lie on the floor
  • Fill bathtubs and containers with water (supply may be cut)
  • Keep shoes on — broken glass is a major hazard
  • Monitor official news channels and embassy communications
  • Do not go outside during active sirens or explosions
  • If you hear a siren in Israel, you have 15–90 seconds to reach shelter (depends on location)
Disclaimer: This checklist provides general safety guidance. Specific situations may require different actions. Always follow instructions from local authorities and your embassy. WithSwag is not a safety authority.
