🚨 Evacuation Routes & Border Crossings

Land borders, sea routes, and transit country options for leaving the conflict zone safely.

Back to Crisis Help Hub
⚠️ Border Status Changes Rapidly
Border crossings can close without notice during active conflict. Always check with your embassy and local authorities before attempting any crossing. Travel in groups when possible and inform someone of your route.

Land Border Crossings

Known land routes out of the conflict zone. Status is subject to rapid change.

🇮🇱 → 🇯🇴

Israel → Jordan (Allenby/King Hussein Bridge)

The main crossing between Israel/West Bank and Jordan. Located near Jericho. Open Sunday–Thursday typically 8am–midnight, Friday–Saturday shorter hours. Expect long waits during crisis. Bring passport, visa if required, and cash (JOD and USD accepted).

⚠️ May close during active military operations. Check with Jordanian embassy.

🇮🇱 → 🇯🇴

Israel → Jordan (Yitzhak Rabin/Wadi Araba)

Southern crossing near Eilat/Aqaba. Less congested than Allenby Bridge. Good option if you're in southern Israel. From Aqaba, you can fly out via Amman or take a bus north.

🇮🇱 → 🇪🇬

Israel → Egypt (Taba Border Crossing)

Located at the southern tip of Israel near Eilat. Crosses into the Sinai Peninsula. From Taba, you can travel to Sharm el-Sheikh or Cairo. Egyptian visa may be available on arrival for some nationalities. Bring USD cash for visa fees.

🇮🇱 → 🇪🇬

Gaza → Egypt (Rafah Crossing)

The only crossing from Gaza to Egypt. Historically subject to frequent closures. Currently extremely restricted. Only available for specific humanitarian cases and with Egyptian/Palestinian coordination.

⚠️ Extremely limited access. Contact your embassy for assistance.

Sea Evacuation Routes

Maritime options when air and land routes are unavailable.

🇱🇧 → 🇨🇾

Lebanon → Cyprus (Sea Route)

Historically used during evacuations. The crossing from Beirut or Tripoli to Larnaca takes approximately 10–12 hours by ferry. During the 2006 conflict, multiple countries organized naval evacuations via this route. Check with your embassy for organized evacuations.

🇮🇱 → 🇨🇾

Israel → Cyprus (Sea Route)

From Haifa port to Larnaca. Not a regular commercial route but has been used in emergencies. Some cruise lines and charter services may operate during evacuations. Contact your embassy for organized departures.

Transit Country Options

Once you've crossed a border, these countries offer good onward travel connections.

🇹🇷

Turkey

Major transit hub. Istanbul has two international airports (IST and SAW) with flights worldwide. Many nationalities get visa-free entry or e-visa. Turkish Airlines has one of the world's largest route networks.

🇨🇾

Cyprus

EU member state. Larnaca and Paphos airports connect to European cities. Many nationalities enter visa-free. Good staging point for onward travel to Europe.

🇬🇷

Greece

EU member. Athens airport has extensive connections. Ferry network connects islands. Visa-free for many nationalities. Good option if coming from Cyprus or Turkey.

🇯🇴

Jordan

Relatively stable neighbor. Queen Alia Airport in Amman has international flights. Visa on arrival available for many nationalities. Royal Jordanian flies to major cities worldwide.

🇦🇪

UAE (Dubai / Abu Dhabi)

Massive expat infrastructure. Dubai and Abu Dhabi airports are among the world's busiest. Emirates and Etihad fly everywhere. Many nationalities get visa on arrival. Excellent hotels, hospitals, and services.

🇸🇦

Saudi Arabia

Riyadh and Jeddah have major international airports. Tourist e-visa now available for many nationalities. Saudia airline has extensive network. Large Indian, Pakistani, and Filipino expat communities.

🇰🇼

Kuwait

Kuwait International Airport connects to Asia, Europe, and Africa. Kuwait Airways and Jazeera Airways operate. Large expat population with established support networks. Visa requirements vary by nationality.

🇴🇲

Oman

Oman maintains a neutral foreign policy, making it one of the more stable options in the Gulf. Muscat airport has good international connections. Oman Air flies to Europe and Asia. Tourist visa available for many nationalities.

Evacuation Safety Tips

  • Carry your passport and copies of all important documents at all times
  • Keep your phone charged and carry a portable power bank
  • Carry cash in USD, EUR, and local currency — ATMs may not work
  • Travel during daylight hours when possible
  • Inform your embassy and family of your planned route
  • Travel in groups — avoid traveling alone
  • Pack light — one bag you can carry easily
  • Have offline maps downloaded (Google Maps, Maps.me)
Disclaimer: Border crossing information can change without notice during active conflicts. Always verify with official sources and your embassy before attempting any crossing. WithSwag provides this information for general awareness only.
