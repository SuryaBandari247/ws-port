Alternative Routes Still Operating

With direct routes suspended, these alternative paths may still be available. Always confirm with the airline before booking.

🇹🇷

Via Istanbul (IST)

Turkish Airlines maintains one of the largest route networks in the region. Istanbul is a major transit hub with connections to Europe, Americas, and Asia. Many displaced travelers are routing through Turkey.

🇨🇾

Via Larnaca, Cyprus (LCA)

Short sea crossing from Lebanon. Cyprus has been used as an evacuation staging point. Flights connect to major European cities. Check ferry availability from Beirut/Tripoli.

🇯🇴

Via Amman, Jordan (AMM)

Queen Alia International Airport remains operational. Land border crossing from Israel (Allenby/King Hussein Bridge) is possible when open. Royal Jordanian flies to many destinations.

🇪🇬

Via Cairo, Egypt (CAI)

Taba border crossing from Israel to Egypt may be open. Cairo has extensive international connections. EgyptAir and other carriers serve global destinations from CAI.

🇬🇷

Via Athens, Greece (ATH)

Aegean Airlines and others connect Athens to the Eastern Mediterranean. Possible ferry connections from some islands. Good onward connections to Europe and beyond.

🇦🇪

Via Dubai/Abu Dhabi (DXB/AUH)

UAE airports may still be operational depending on airspace status. Emirates and Etihad have massive global networks. Check if Gulf airspace is open before booking.

🇸🇦

Via Riyadh/Jeddah (RUH/JED)

Saudi Arabia's two main airports. Saudia (Saudi Airlines) connects to 100+ destinations. Jeddah is closer to the Red Sea and Africa. Riyadh has more Asian and European connections.

🇰🇼

Via Kuwait (KWI)

Kuwait International Airport. Kuwait Airways and Jazeera Airways operate international routes. Good option for connecting to South Asia, Europe, and other Gulf states.

🇴🇲

Via Muscat, Oman (MCT)

Muscat International Airport. Oman Air connects to Europe, Asia, and Africa. Oman has historically maintained neutral relations, making it a relatively stable transit option.

💡 Tips for Finding Cheaper Flights During a Crisis

  • Book one-way tickets — round trips may lock you into return dates that become impossible
  • Check nearby airports (e.g., Amman instead of Tel Aviv, Larnaca instead of Beirut)
  • Use Google Flights "Explore" to find the cheapest destination from your location
  • Set price alerts on Skyscanner — prices fluctuate hourly during crises
  • Consider budget carriers like Pegasus (Turkey), Wizz Air, or Ryanair for European legs
  • Contact your airline directly — they may offer free rebooking during conflict situations
  • Check if your travel insurance covers conflict-zone evacuations

Airport Status Overview

Current operational status of key airports in the region. Status can change rapidly — verify before traveling.

🇮🇱 Ben Gurion (TLV)

Israel's main international airport. Operations may be disrupted during active conflict. Many international carriers have suspended service. Check with your airline directly.

🇱🇧 Beirut (BEY)

Rafic Hariri International Airport. Operations heavily affected by regional conflict. Limited carriers may still operate. Middle East Airlines (MEA) is the primary carrier.

🇯🇴 Amman (AMM)

Queen Alia International Airport. Generally more stable. Royal Jordanian and international carriers operate. Key alternative for those who can reach Jordan by land.

🇮🇷 Tehran (IKA)

Imam Khomeini International Airport. Iranian airspace closures directly affect operations. Very limited international service during active conflict.

🇦🇪 Dubai (DXB)

The world's busiest international airport. Emirates hub. Typically remains operational but may face disruptions if Gulf airspace is restricted. Massive global connectivity.

🇦🇪 Abu Dhabi (AUH)

Etihad Airways hub. Modern facility with global connections. Status depends on Gulf airspace availability. Good alternative if Dubai is congested.

🇸🇦 Riyadh (RUH)

King Khalid International Airport. Saudia hub. Saudi airspace may be affected by regional conflict. Check airline status before booking.

🇸🇦 Jeddah (JED)

King Abdulaziz International Airport. Western Saudi Arabia, closer to the Red Sea. May be less affected by eastern Gulf airspace closures. Good for Africa and Europe connections.

🇰🇼 Kuwait (KWI)

Kuwait International Airport. Closer to Iraq and Iran, so more likely to be affected by airspace closures. Kuwait Airways and Jazeera Airways operate from here.

🇴🇲 Muscat (MCT)

Muscat International Airport. Oman's neutral stance may help keep this airport operational. Oman Air connects to major cities. Good fallback if other Gulf airports are disrupted.
