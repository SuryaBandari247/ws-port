    When Do You Need Photos for H1B?

    1. H1B Petition (Form I-129)

    Your employer files Form I-129 with USCIS. This requires 2 printed passport-style photos of the beneficiary (you). Photos must be:

    • 2×2 inches, white background
    • Taken within the last 6 months
    • Attached to Form I-129 with your name and A-number (if any) written on the back

    2. H1B Visa Stamping (Embassy/Consulate)

    When you go for visa stamping at a US embassy or consulate, you need:

    • Digital photo uploaded to DS-160 (600×600px JPEG)
    • 2 printed photos (2×2 inches) to bring to the interview
    • Photos must be recent — taken within 6 months

    3. H1B Extension or Transfer

    For H1B extensions (Form I-129) and employer transfers, you need 2 new photos. Even if your appearance hasn't changed, USCIS requires recent photos.

    4. H1B to Green Card (I-485)

    If you're adjusting status from H1B to permanent resident, Form I-485 requires 2 photos. See our Green Card photo guide for details.

    Important: A non-compliant photo can trigger a Request for Evidence (RFE) from USCIS, delaying your H1B processing by 2–4 months. This is especially critical during H1B cap season when timing matters.

    Photo Requirements in Detail

    Face and Expression

    Face clearly visible and centered in the frame. Neutral expression with both eyes open and mouth closed. Both ears visible. Full head from top of hair to chin.

    Background

    Plain white or off-white. No patterns, shadows, or other objects. Stand about 4 feet from a white wall with even lighting to avoid shadows.

    Glasses

    No glasses of any kind since November 2016. Remove all eyeglasses before taking the photo.

    Clothing

    Normal business or casual clothing. Avoid white shirts that blend with the background. No uniforms. No hats or head coverings (religious exceptions apply with full face visible).

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    1. Using an old photo — Must be within 6 months, even for extensions
    2. Wrong DS-160 file size — Must be 54KB–10MB JPEG
    3. Shadows on background — Stand away from the wall
    4. Wearing glasses — Not allowed since 2016
    5. Selfie with wrong dimensions — Must be square (2×2 or 600×600)
    6. Photo printed on regular paper — Use glossy photo paper
    7. Writing on the front — Write name/A-number on the back only

    How to Take Your H1B Photo at Home

    1. Find a white wall with good natural lighting
    2. Stand 4 feet from the wall
    3. Have someone take the photo at eye level (or use a tripod)
    4. Use your phone's rear camera for best quality
    5. Take several shots, pick the best one
    6. Upload to WithSwag — select US Passport/Visa size
    7. Download both digital (for DS-160) and print versions
    8. Print 2 copies on glossy 4×6 photo paper
