    What You Need

    • A smartphone (iPhone or Android) — rear camera preferred
    • A plain white or light-colored wall
    • Natural daylight (a window works great)
    • A friend to take the photo, or a tripod with timer
    • WithSwag's free passport photo tool to crop and format

    Step 1: Set Up Your Background

    Find a plain white wall in your home. The wall should be clean, with no pictures, nails, or visible textures. If you don't have a white wall, tape a large white sheet, poster board, or bedsheet to the wall.

    Pro tip: Don't worry if your wall isn't perfectly white. WithSwag's AI background removal can replace any background with the exact color your country requires.

    Step 2: Get the Lighting Right

    Lighting is the #1 factor in getting a good passport photo. Here's how to nail it:

    • Use natural light. Stand facing a large window. The light should fall evenly on your face.
    • Avoid direct sunlight. It creates harsh shadows. Diffused light from a cloudy day or a north-facing window is ideal.
    • Don't use flash. Flash causes red eye, harsh shadows, and uneven lighting.
    • Avoid overhead lighting only. It creates shadows under your eyes and nose.
    • Check for shadows on the wall behind you. Stand at least 3–4 feet from the wall.

    Step 3: Position Yourself

    • Stand 3–4 feet in front of the wall
    • Face the camera directly — not at an angle
    • Keep your shoulders level and relaxed
    • The camera should be at your eye level
    • The photographer should stand about 4 feet away

    Step 4: Get Your Expression Right

    • Neutral expression — no smiling, no frowning
    • Both eyes open and looking directly at the camera
    • Mouth closed
    • Remove glasses (required for US, UK, and many other countries)
    • Remove hats and head coverings (unless religious)
    • Hair should not cover your face or eyes

    Step 5: Take Multiple Shots

    Take at least 5–10 photos. Check each one for:

    • Even lighting with no shadows on face or background
    • Sharp focus on the face
    • Eyes open, neutral expression
    • Head centered in the frame

    Step 6: Format Your Photo Online

    1. Go to withswag.org/portrait
    2. Upload your best photo
    3. Select your country's passport size (US 2×2", UK 35×45mm, etc.)
    4. Use the smart crop tool to frame your face correctly
    5. Use AI background removal if your wall wasn't perfectly white
    6. Adjust brightness and contrast if needed
    7. Download in high resolution (300 DPI)
