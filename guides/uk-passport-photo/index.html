    Key Differences from Other Countries

    The UK has some unique requirements that catch people off guard:

    • Background is NOT white — HMPO requires light grey or cream, not pure white. This is different from the US and most other countries.
    • No glasses at all — Since 2018, glasses are completely banned in UK passport photos, even prescription ones.
    • Stricter on expression — Absolutely neutral. Even a slight smile can cause rejection.
    Common mistake: Using a pure white background. UK passport photos specifically need light grey or cream. WithSwag has the exact UK-compliant background color built in.

    Digital Photo Requirements for Online Applications

    If applying online through GOV.UK, your digital photo must meet these additional specs:

    • Minimum 600 pixels wide × 750 pixels tall
    • File size between 50 KB and 10 MB
    • No filters, no editing of facial features
    • Clear and in focus with no red eye

    Taking a UK Passport Photo at Home

    1. Stand in front of a plain, light-colored wall
    2. Use natural daylight — avoid direct flash
    3. Keep a neutral expression, mouth closed, eyes open
    4. Remove glasses, hats, and head coverings (unless religious)
