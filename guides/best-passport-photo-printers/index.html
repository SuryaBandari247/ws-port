What Makes a Good Passport Photo Printer?

Not every printer can produce passport-quality photos. Here's what matters:

  • Minimum 300 DPI print resolution (most photo printers do 4800+ DPI)
  • Accurate color reproduction — skin tones must look natural
  • Glossy or semi-glossy output on photo paper
  • Borderless printing capability (for 4×6 sheets)
  • Consistent results — every print should look identical

How to Print Passport Photos at Home

  1. Create your passport photo at WithSwag — select your country and document type
  2. Choose "Photo Collage" to fit multiple photos on one 4×6 sheet
  3. Download the 300 DPI print-ready file
  4. Load glossy photo paper into your printer
  5. Print at "Best Quality" or "Photo" setting — make sure scaling is set to 100% (actual size)
  6. Let the print dry for 1-2 minutes
  7. Cut individual photos using a paper cutter or ruler + craft knife
No printer? Download the 4×6 collage from WithSwag and print it at any drugstore photo kiosk (CVS, Walgreens, Walmart) for about $0.35. You get 4+ passport photos for under $4 total.
