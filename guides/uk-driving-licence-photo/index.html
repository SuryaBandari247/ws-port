    Photo Specifications

    SpecificationRequirement
    Photo size35 × 45 mm
    Head height (chin to crown)29–34 mm
    BackgroundPlain light grey or cream
    ExpressionNeutral, mouth closed
    GlassesNot allowed (since Nov 2016)
    Head coveringsNot allowed (religious exceptions)
    RecencyWithin the last month (new), 5 years (renewal)
    Print qualityProfessional quality, no creases or marks
    Key fact: UK driving licence photos follow exactly the same rules as UK passport photos. If you have a recent passport photo, you can use it for your DVLA application.

    Full Licence vs. Provisional Licence

    The photo requirements are identical for both:

    • Provisional driving licence — First application, need a new photo
    • Full driving licence — After passing your test, same photo specs
    • Renewal — Every 10 years, new photo required
    • Replacement — If lost or stolen, new photo needed

    How to Apply

    Online (GOV.UK)

    Apply at gov.uk. You can upload a digital photo or use your passport photo from the HMPO database.

    By Post (D1 Form)

    Send a paper application with two identical passport-standard photos. Photos must be 35×45mm, printed on photo-quality paper.

    Common Rejection Reasons

    1. Glasses worn — Not allowed since 2016
    2. Wrong background — Must be light grey or cream, not white
    3. Smiling — Neutral expression required
    4. Shadows on face or background — Even lighting needed
    5. Photo too old — Must be recent
    6. Red eye — Not acceptable
    7. Low quality print — Must be sharp, on photo paper
