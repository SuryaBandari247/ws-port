    Employment-Based Green Card: Step by Step

    This is the most common path for H1B holders and skilled workers. There are three main stages.

    Stage 1: PERM Labor Certification

    Your employer must prove that no qualified US worker is available for the position.

    • Employer conducts recruitment (job ads, resume review, interviews)
    • Recruitment period: 30-60 days minimum
    • Employer files ETA Form 9089 with Department of Labor
    • Processing time: 6-18 months (varies significantly)
    • Cost: $0 government fee (employer pays attorney + recruitment costs)
    Important: The PERM process is employer-driven. If you change employers, you typically need to restart PERM from scratch (unless you have an approved I-140 and can port your priority date).

    Stage 2: I-140 Immigrant Petition

    After PERM approval, your employer files Form I-140 with USCIS.

    DetailInfo
    FormI-140 (Immigrant Petition for Alien Workers)
    Filing fee$700
    Premium processing$2,805 (15 business day response)
    Regular processing6-12 months
    Priority dateSet by PERM filing date (this is your "place in line")
    Key concept — Priority Date: Your priority date is the date your PERM application was filed. This determines your place in the queue. You can only file I-485 (final stage) when your priority date becomes "current" in the Visa Bulletin.

    Stage 3: I-485 Adjustment of Status (or Consular Processing)

    The final step — applying for the actual Green Card. You can only file when your priority date is current.

    If you're in the US, file I-485 (Adjustment of Status):

    • Form I-485 filing fee: $1,440 (includes biometrics)
    • Medical exam (Form I-693): $200-$500
    • 2 passport-style photos (2×2 inch, white background)
    • Processing time: 8-24 months after filing
    • You get an EAD (work permit) and Advance Parole while waiting

    If you're outside the US, go through consular processing:

    • Interview at US consulate in your home country
    • DS-260 immigrant visa application
    • Medical exam by approved physician
    • 2×2 inch passport photos

    EB-2 NIW (National Interest Waiver)

    The NIW is a special path within EB-2 that doesn't require employer sponsorship or PERM labor certification. You self-petition by proving your work is in the national interest of the United States.

    • No employer sponsorship needed — you file yourself
    • No PERM labor certification required
    • Must prove: advanced degree + national interest
    • Popular with researchers, scientists, engineers, healthcare workers
    • Filing fee: $700 (I-140) + attorney fees ($5,000-$10,000)

    Key Documents for Green Card Application

    • Valid passport
    • Birth certificate (with certified translation if not in English)
    • Marriage certificate (if applicable)
    • 2×2 inch passport photos (USCIS specifications)
    • Form I-693 medical exam results
    • Tax returns (last 3 years)
    • Employment verification letters
    • Degree certificates and transcripts
    • Police clearance certificates
    • I-94 arrival/departure record
