What is the EU Blue Card?

The EU Blue Card is a work and residence permit for highly qualified non-EU nationals. It's the EU's answer to the US Green Card for skilled workers. Since the revised directive (2021/1883) took effect in November 2023, the Blue Card has become more accessible with lower salary thresholds and broader eligibility.

Germany issues the vast majority of EU Blue Cards — over 70,000 per year — followed by France, Poland, and the Netherlands. Every Blue Card application requires biometric photos.

🇩🇪 Germany — Blaue Karte EU Photo

Germany is by far the largest issuer of EU Blue Cards. The photo requirements are identical to German passport/residence permit photos — strict biometric standards apply.

German Blue Card Photo Specs

SpecificationRequirement
Photo size35 × 45 mm
Face height (chin to crown)32–36 mm
BackgroundLight grey (uniform, no patterns)
BiometricYes — strict ICAO compliance
ExpressionNeutral, mouth closed, no smile
GlassesNot allowed (since 2017)
RecencyWithin 6 months
Quantity1 biometric photo

Where to Submit

  • Initial application: German embassy/consulate in your home country, or Ausländerbehörde if already in Germany on a different visa
  • The photo is used for your electronic residence permit card (eAT)
  • Some Ausländerbehörden take the photo on-site; others require you to bring one
Important: German biometric photo standards are among the strictest in Europe. The Ausländerbehörde may reject photos that don't precisely match the Fotomustertafel. WithSwag ensures exact compliance.

Documents That Require the Same Photo

Once you have a compliant biometric photo, you can often reuse it for related applications:

  • Blue Card application — initial work permit
  • Residence permit renewal — same photo specs
  • Family reunification — spouse/children joining you
  • Permanent residence — after 21–33 months with Blue Card
  • Naturalisation — citizenship application (same photo specs)
  • National ID card — if applicable in your country
Tip: With the revised Blue Card directive, you can apply for permanent residence after just 21 months (if you have B1 language skills) or 33 months. Keep extra copies of your biometric photo for future applications.
