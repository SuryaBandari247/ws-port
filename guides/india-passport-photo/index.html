    Indian Passport Photo Specifications

    SpecificationRequirement
    Photo size2 × 2 inches (51 × 51 mm)
    Face coverage70–80% of the frame
    Background colorPlain white or light-colored
    ResolutionMinimum 300 DPI for print
    File format (digital)JPEG
    File size (digital)10 KB to 300 KB
    Pixel dimensions (digital)350 × 350 minimum
    RecencyTaken within the last 6 months
    Official Source: Passport Seva (Ministry of External Affairs) — Photo Specifications (PDF)

    Detailed Requirements

    Face and Expression

    The photo must show your full face from the front. Keep a neutral expression with mouth closed. Both eyes must be open and clearly visible. The face should take up 70–80% of the photo area.

    Background

    A plain white background is preferred by Indian passport authorities. Light-colored backgrounds (very light grey or cream) are also accepted. The background must be uniform with no patterns, objects, or shadows.

    Tip: For Indian passport applications through the Passport Seva portal, you'll need a digital photo. Use WithSwag to create both print and digital versions in one go.

    Glasses and Accessories

    Remove glasses if possible. If you must wear prescription glasses, ensure there is no glare or reflection on the lenses. Sunglasses and tinted lenses are not allowed. Remove hats and head coverings unless worn for religious reasons.

    OCI Card Photo Requirements

    Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) card photos follow the same specifications as Indian passport photos: 2×2 inches, white background, front-facing. The photo must be recent (within 6 months) and match your current appearance.

    Indian Visa Photo Requirements

    Indian tourist and business visa photos also use the 2×2 inch format. The requirements are identical to passport photos. Some consulates may accept 2×2.5 inch photos, but 2×2 is universally accepted.

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    • Using a photo with shadows on the face or background
    • Submitting a photo older than 6 months
    • Face too small in the frame (must be 70–80%)
    • Wearing white clothing that blends with the background
    • Digital file too large or too small for online applications
    • Photo taken with filters or heavy editing
