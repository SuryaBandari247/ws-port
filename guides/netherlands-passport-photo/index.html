    Detailed Requirements

    Face and Expression

    The Netherlands is very strict about facial expression. Your face must show a completely neutral expression — no smile, no raised eyebrows, mouth closed. Both eyes must be open and looking directly at the camera. The face must be evenly lit without shadows on either side.

    Both Ears Visible

    A distinctive Dutch requirement is that both ears must be clearly visible. Hair must be tucked behind the ears or styled so it doesn't cover them. This is strictly enforced at the gemeente (municipality) when you submit your photo.

    Important: The "both ears visible" rule is one of the most common reasons Dutch passport photos get rejected. Make sure to pull hair back from both ears before taking your photo.

    Background

    The Netherlands requires a uniform light grey background — pure white is not accepted. The background must be completely even with no gradients, shadows, or patterns. This is checked carefully by gemeente staff.

    Glasses Policy

    The Netherlands was one of the first European countries to ban glasses in passport photos, implementing the rule in 2007. All types of eyewear must be removed, including prescription glasses, sunglasses, and tinted lenses.

    Children's Photos

    Children under 6 years old have slightly relaxed requirements — the mouth may be slightly open and the expression doesn't need to be perfectly neutral. However, the child must be the only person in the photo (no parent holding them visible).

    What Documents Use This Photo?

    • Nederlands paspoort — Dutch passport
    • Identiteitskaart (ID-kaart) — National ID card
    • Verblijfsvergunning — Residence permit
    • Schengen-visum — Schengen visa applications through the Netherlands
    • Rijbewijs — Driving license

    Common Rejection Reasons

    1. Ears not visible — Both ears must be fully visible
    2. White background — Must be light grey
    3. Glasses worn — Not allowed since 2007
    4. Expression not neutral — No smile at all
    5. Shadows on face or background — Must be evenly lit
    6. Face too small or too large — Must be 26–30mm chin to crown
    7. Photo too old — Must be within 6 months
    8. Head tilted — Must be perfectly straight
