    Photo Requirements in Detail

    Face and Expression

    Japan has very strict face positioning rules. The face must be centered, with a neutral expression. Both eyes must be open and clearly visible. Mouth must be closed. Hair must not cover the eyes or eyebrows.

    Background

    Plain white background with no shadows, patterns, or gradients. Japan is stricter than many countries about background uniformity.

    Glasses

    Since 2021, Japan does not accept passport photos with any type of glasses — prescription, sunglasses, or tinted lenses. Remove all eyewear.

    Head Coverings

    No hats, caps, or head coverings unless worn for religious reasons. Even then, the full face from hairline to chin must be visible.

    Common Rejection Reasons

    1. Face too large or too small — Must be exactly 34mm ± 2mm
    2. Glasses in photo — Not allowed since 2021
    3. Shadows on background — Background must be uniformly white
    4. Hair covering eyes — Both eyes and eyebrows must be fully visible
    5. Smiling or open mouth — Neutral expression only
    6. Photo too old — Must be within 6 months

    How to Take a Japan Passport Photo at Home

    1. Stand in front of a plain white wall with even lighting
    2. Remove glasses, hats, and any accessories
    3. Keep a neutral expression with mouth closed
    4. Have someone take the photo at eye level
    5. Upload to WithSwag and select "Japan" as the country
    6. Download the 35×45mm formatted photo

    Japan Visa Photo

    Japan visa photos use the same 35×45mm format as passport photos. The same photo works for both applications.
