Detailed Requirements

Face and Expression

Norway follows strict biometric standards. Your face must be centered and occupy 70–80% of the photo. Expression must be completely neutral with mouth closed. Both eyes must be open and clearly visible, looking directly at the camera. The face must be evenly illuminated without shadows.

Background

Norway requires a uniform light grey background. Pure white is not accepted. The background must be completely even — no gradients, shadows, or patterns. This is one of the most common rejection reasons for Norwegian passport photos.

Tip: Norway's police (politiet) handle passport applications and are strict about photo quality. WithSwag applies the exact light grey background tone that meets Norwegian requirements, avoiding the most common rejection reason.

Glasses Policy

Since 2018, glasses are not allowed in Norwegian passport and ID photos. All eyewear must be removed, including prescription glasses. This aligns with the broader European trend toward stricter biometric standards.

Head Coverings

Head coverings are not permitted unless worn for religious reasons. The face must be fully visible from chin to forehead, and both sides of the face must be clearly visible.

Norwegian National ID Card

Norway introduced a national ID card (nasjonalt ID-kort) in 2020. The photo requirements are identical to passport photos — 35×45mm, light grey background, biometric-compliant.

What Documents Use This Photo?

  • Norsk pass — Norwegian passport
  • Nasjonalt ID-kort — National ID card
  • Oppholdstillatelse — Residence permit
  • Schengen-visum — Schengen visa applications through Norway
  • Førerkort — Driving license

Common Rejection Reasons

  1. Wrong background color — Must be light grey, not white
  2. Glasses worn — Not allowed since 2018
  3. Shadows on face — Lighting must be completely even
  4. Face too small or too large — Must be 32–36mm chin to crown
  5. Expression not neutral — No smile, mouth closed
  6. Head tilted or turned — Must face camera directly
  7. Photo too old — Must be within 6 months
  8. Low resolution or blurry — Must be sharp and clear
