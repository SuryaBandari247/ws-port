Why Background Color Matters

Passport offices use automated systems to verify photos. These systems check background color, uniformity, and contrast against your face. Using the wrong color is one of the top reasons for photo rejection.

How to Get the Right Background

Option 1: Use the Right Wall

Stand in front of a plain wall that matches your country's requirement. For white backgrounds, a clean white wall works. For light grey, a light-colored wall may work.

Option 2: AI Background Removal (Recommended)

The easiest approach: take your photo in front of any background, then use WithSwag's AI background removal to automatically replace it with the exact color your country needs. This guarantees a perfectly uniform background with no shadows.

Common Background Mistakes

  • Using white for UK photos — UK requires light grey, not white
  • Shadows on the background — Stand 3-4 feet from the wall
  • Patterned wallpaper visible — Must be completely plain
  • Other people or objects in background — Must be completely empty
  • Uneven color — Background must be uniform across the entire photo
