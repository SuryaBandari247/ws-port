How to Create Subtitles for YouTube Videos (Free)

Make SRT or SBV subtitle files and upload them to YouTube Studio. No software to install.

Open Subtitle Editor →

Why Add Subtitles to Your YouTube Videos?

  • Reach more viewers — 85% of Facebook videos are watched without sound. YouTube is similar.
  • Better SEO — YouTube indexes subtitle text, helping your video rank for more keywords
  • Accessibility — Makes your content accessible to deaf and hard-of-hearing viewers
  • International audience — Subtitles can be auto-translated by YouTube into 100+ languages
  • Higher watch time — Viewers with subtitles watch longer on average

Step 1: Create Your Subtitle File

  1. Go to WithSwag's Subtitle Editor
  2. Choose "Create Manually" mode
  3. Watch your video and note the timestamps for each line of dialogue
  4. Enter each subtitle with its start time, end time, and text
  5. Use auto-increment to speed up the process
Tip: Enable "Auto-increment timestamps" and set the duration to match your average subtitle length (2–3 seconds). This saves a lot of time.

Step 2: Export in the Right Format

YouTube accepts these subtitle formats:

  • SBV — YouTube's native format (recommended)
  • SRT — Most widely used, works great with YouTube
  • VTT — Also accepted

In WithSwag's subtitle editor, use the Format Conversion tool to export as SBV or SRT.

Step 3: Upload to YouTube Studio

  1. Go to YouTube Studio → select your video
  2. Click Subtitles in the left menu
  3. Click Add Language → select your language
  4. Click Add under "Subtitles" → choose "Upload file"
  5. Select "With timing"
  6. Upload your SRT or SBV file
  7. Review the subtitles in the editor and click Publish

Tips for Better YouTube Subtitles

  • Keep each subtitle under 2 lines and under 42 characters per line
  • Display each subtitle for 1–7 seconds
  • Don't overlap subtitle timings
  • Include relevant sound effects in brackets: [music], [applause], [door closes]
  • Break at natural pauses in speech, not mid-sentence

Fixing Subtitle Timing

If your subtitles are out of sync with the video, use WithSwag's Time Shift tool:

  • Subtitles appear too early? Use a positive shift (e.g., +1.5 seconds)
  • Subtitles appear too late? Use a negative shift (e.g., -1.5 seconds)
  • Video speed changed? Use the Speed Adjustment tool (e.g., 0.5x for a 2x speed video)
