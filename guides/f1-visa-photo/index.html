F1 Visa Application — When You Need Photos

1. DS-160 Online Application

Every F1 visa applicant must complete the DS-160 form online. During the form, you'll upload a digital photo:

  • 600×600 to 1200×1200 pixels, JPEG format
  • 54KB to 10MB file size
  • The system validates your photo automatically

2. Embassy/Consulate Interview

Bring 2 printed photos (2×2 inches) to your visa interview. Some embassies have photo booths, but they're expensive ($10–$20) and you may have to wait in line.

3. University ID (After Arrival)

Many US universities use a passport-style photo for your student ID. Having extra copies of your visa photo can save time during orientation.

Tip for international students: Your home country's passport photo size may be different from the US 2×2 inch requirement. European photos are 35×45mm, Indian photos are 2×2 inches (same), Chinese photos are 33×48mm. Use WithSwag to get the exact US size regardless of where you're from.

Common Mistakes for F1 Applicants

  1. Using home country passport photo — Wrong size for most countries
  2. HEIC file from iPhone — DS-160 needs JPEG; convert first
  3. Photo taken in a photo booth abroad — Often wrong dimensions
  4. Wearing glasses — Not allowed since 2016
  5. Smiling — Must be neutral expression
  6. Photo older than 6 months — Must be recent
  7. Shadows or non-white background — Must be clean white

How to Take Your F1 Photo at Home

  1. Find a white wall with good natural light
  2. Stand 4 feet from the wall to avoid shadows
  3. Have a friend take the photo at eye level
  4. Use your phone's rear camera
  5. Take several shots, pick the best
  6. Upload to WithSwag — select US Passport/Visa size
