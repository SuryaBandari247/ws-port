    Why Is Travel Insurance Mandatory?

    The Schengen Borders Code (Regulation EC 810/2009) requires all short-stay visa applicants to hold travel medical insurance. This isn't optional — your application will be rejected without it. The requirement exists because Schengen countries want to ensure visitors can cover medical costs without burdening European healthcare systems.

Generic "worldwide" policies without specific Schengen mention may be rejected.

    What Consulates Check

    When reviewing your insurance document, consulate staff verify:

    1. Coverage amount is at least €30,000
    2. Policy covers all Schengen countries (not just one)
    3. Dates cover your entire stay (some want +15 days buffer)
    4. Emergency evacuation and repatriation are included
    5. The insurance company is reputable and recognized
    6. The policy document is in English or the local language
    Pro tip: Buy your insurance at least a few days before your visa appointment. Print the full policy document — not just the confirmation email. Some consulates want to see the detailed terms and conditions.

    What If My Visa Is Denied?

    All three providers listed above offer refunds if your Schengen visa is denied. The process:

    1. Contact the insurance provider with your visa denial letter
    2. Submit a cancellation/refund request
    3. Receive a full or partial refund (depends on provider and timing)

    Common Mistakes to Avoid

    • Buying insurance that doesn't specifically mention Schengen coverage
    • Coverage amount below €30,000
    • Policy dates that don't cover your full stay
    • Forgetting to print the policy document
    • Using a credit card's built-in travel insurance (usually doesn't meet Schengen requirements)
    • Buying from an unknown provider that consulates don't recognize
    • Booking flights via London without a UK Transit Visa — Asian passport holders (India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Philippines, China, etc.) need a separate UK transit visa even for airside connections
    ⚠️ UK Transit Warning for Asian Passport Holders: If you hold a passport from India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Sri Lanka, Philippines, China, or most other Asian countries, you cannot transit through London (Heathrow, Gatwick) without a UK Direct Airside Transit Visa (DATV) — even if you're just changing planes and never leave the airport. A Schengen visa does NOT exempt you. Book direct flights to your Schengen destination, or transit through Amsterdam (AMS), Frankfurt (FRA), Paris (CDG), or Istanbul (IST) instead. Being denied boarding costs you the flight and can jeopardize your visa application.
