🇺🇸 United States — N-400 Naturalisation Photo

When applying for US citizenship through Form N-400 (Application for Naturalization), you must submit 2 identical passport-style photographs. The specs are the same as a US passport photo.

US Naturalisation Photo Specs

SpecificationRequirement
Photo size2 × 2 inches (51 × 51 mm)
Head height (chin to crown)1 – 1⅜ inches (25–35 mm)
BackgroundPlain white
ExpressionNeutral, mouth closed
GlassesNot allowed
RecencyWithin 30 days of filing
Quantity2 identical photos

Important Notes for N-400

  • Write your A-Number (Alien Registration Number) lightly in pencil on the back of each photo
  • Do not staple or paperclip photos to the application
  • Photos must be printed on thin photo-quality paper
  • If you wear a head covering for religious reasons, your face must still be fully visible from the bottom of the chin to the top of the forehead
Official Source: USCIS — Form N-400 Instructions | Photo specs follow U.S. Department of State guidelines

🇬🇧 United Kingdom — Naturalisation (Form AN) Photo

UK naturalisation applications (Form AN for adults, Form MN1 for minors) require 2 identical passport-style photographs. One photo must be countersigned by a referee who can confirm your identity.

UK Naturalisation Photo Specs

SpecificationRequirement
Photo size35 × 45 mm
Head height (chin to crown)29–34 mm
BackgroundPlain light grey or cream
ExpressionNeutral, mouth closed
GlassesNot allowed
RecencyWithin the last month
Quantity2 (one countersigned on the back)

Countersigning Requirements

  • One photo must be signed on the back by your referee
  • The referee writes: "I certify that this is a true likeness of [your full name]"
  • The referee must be a professional person (doctor, lawyer, teacher, etc.) who has known you for at least 3 years
  • The referee must be a British citizen or hold a professional position in the UK
Official Source: GOV.UK — Apply for British citizenship | Photo requirements

🇩🇪 Germany — Einbürgerung (Naturalisation) Photo

German naturalisation (Einbürgerung) requires biometric photos identical to those used for the German passport (Reisepass) or ID card (Personalausweis). The application is handled by your local Einbürgerungsbehörde (naturalisation authority).

German Naturalisation Photo Specs

SpecificationRequirement
Photo size35 × 45 mm
Face height (chin to crown)32–36 mm
BackgroundLight grey (uniform, no patterns)
BiometricYes — strict ICAO compliance
ExpressionNeutral, mouth closed, no smile
GlassesNot allowed (since 2017)
Quantity1–2 (varies by Bundesland)
Note: Germany has some of the strictest biometric photo requirements in Europe. Photos from automated booths are often rejected. Consider using WithSwag to ensure exact compliance with the Fotomustertafel (official photo template).
Official Source: BMI (Federal Ministry of the Interior) — Fotomustertafel (Photo Template)

🇫🇷 France — Naturalisation Photo

French naturalisation (naturalisation par décret or par déclaration) requires photos that meet the same ANTS (Agence Nationale des Titres Sécurisés) standards as French passport photos.

France Naturalisation Photo Specs

SpecificationRequirement

Face height32–36 mm
BackgroundLight grey or light blue
ExpressionNeutral, mouth closed
GlassesNot allowed
Quantity2–3 (check your préfecture)

🇳🇱 Netherlands — Naturalisatie Photo

SpecificationRequirement

Both earsMust be visible

🇨🇦 Canada — Citizenship Application Photo

Canadian citizenship applications require 2 identical photos that meet the same specifications as Canadian passport photos. The photos must be taken by a commercial photographer.

Canada Citizenship Photo Specs

SpecificationRequirement
Photo size50 × 70 mm
Face height (chin to crown)31–36 mm
BackgroundPlain white or light-coloured
ExpressionNeutral, mouth closed
GlassesNot allowed
RecencyWithin the last 12 months
Quantity2 identical

Canadian Citizenship Photo Rules

  • One photo must have the photographer's name, address, and date taken on the back
  • The other photo must have your name and date of birth on the back
  • Photos must be taken by a commercial photographer (not self-taken)
  • Black and white or colour photos are both accepted
Official Source: Government of Canada (IRCC) — Photo specifications | Citizenship application guide

🇮🇳 India — Citizenship / OCI Photo

India does not have a traditional naturalisation pathway for most applicants, but Overseas Citizenship of India (OCI) card applications require passport-style photos. Indian citizenship by registration or naturalisation (under the Citizenship Act, 1955) also requires photos.

India OCI / Citizenship Photo Specs

SpecificationRequirement
Photo size51 × 51 mm (2 × 2 inches)
Face coverage50–69% of frame
BackgroundPlain white
ExpressionNeutral, mouth closed
GlassesNot recommended
Quantity2–4 (varies by application type)

Naturalisation Photo vs. Passport Photo — Key Differences

AspectPassport PhotoNaturalisation Photo
Photo specsCountry standardSame as passport (in most countries)
Number of copiesUsually 1–2Often 2–4
CountersigningSometimes requiredOften required (UK, Australia, Canada)
Writing on backVariesOften required (name, A-number, date)
Recency1–6 monthsUsually within 30 days – 6 months

Common Rejection Reasons for Naturalisation Photos

  1. Photo too old — Must be recent (check your country's specific timeframe)
  2. Wrong background colour — Must match country requirements exactly
  3. Missing countersignature — Required in UK, Australia, and Canada
  4. Wrong size — Must be exact dimensions for your country
  5. Glasses worn — Most countries now prohibit glasses
  6. Shadows or uneven lighting — Must be evenly lit
  7. Expression not neutral — No smiling, mouth must be closed
  8. Wrong number of copies — Submit the exact number required
