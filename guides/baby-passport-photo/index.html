    Why Baby Passport Photos Are Difficult

    Babies don't cooperate. They squirm, close their eyes, cry, and grab things. Passport offices have strict rules — even for infants — so you need patience and the right technique.

    The good news: you can take a perfect baby passport photo at home in about 10 minutes. Most parents get a usable shot within 15–20 attempts.

    How to Take a Baby Passport Photo at Home

    Method 1: Lay Baby on White Sheet (Best for Newborns)

    This is the easiest method and works for babies 0–12 months.

    1. Lay a plain white sheet or blanket on a bed or the floor — smooth out all wrinkles
    2. Place the baby face-up on the sheet
    3. Remove all hats, headbands, pacifiers, bibs, and toys
    4. Stand directly above the baby with your phone camera pointing straight down
    5. Use natural light from a nearby window — no flash
    6. Take 20+ photos rapidly — you need one with eyes open and mouth closed
    7. Pick the best shot and upload to WithSwag
    Pro tip: Feed the baby right before the photo session. A full, content baby is much more likely to stay calm with eyes open.

    Method 2: Car Seat Method (Good for 3–12 Months)

    1. Place the baby in their car seat (reclined position)
    2. Drape a white sheet over the car seat behind the baby's head
    3. Position at eye level and photograph from the front
    4. Make sure no car seat straps or edges are visible

    Method 3: Parent Holds from Behind (Toddlers 1–3 Years)

    1. Have one parent hold the child facing the camera
    2. The parent wears a white shirt and stands against a white wall
    3. The parent's hands and body must not be visible in the final crop
    4. Another person takes the photo from the front

    Common Mistakes (and How to Avoid Them)

    1. Shadows on face — Use soft natural light, never flash. Position near a window.
    2. Eyes closed — Take 20+ photos in burst mode. Gently call the baby's name.
    3. Pacifier in mouth — Remove everything from the baby's mouth and hands.
    4. Parent's hands visible — Use the lay-down method to avoid this entirely.
    5. Wrinkled background — Iron or stretch the white sheet flat before placing baby.
    6. Baby looking sideways — Hold a toy or make a sound directly above the camera.
    7. Too far away or too close — WithSwag auto-crops, so just make sure the full head is visible.
    Don't use Snapchat or Instagram filters. Any digital alteration, smoothing, or filter will get the photo rejected. Use your phone's default camera app.

    Lighting Tips for Baby Photos

    • Natural daylight from a window is best — overcast days give the softest, most even light
    • Never use flash — it creates harsh shadows and can startle the baby
    • Avoid direct sunlight — it causes squinting and uneven shadows
    • If the room is dark, turn on all overhead lights and position near the brightest window
